I am a computational scientist with a Ph.D. in Chemistry from National Tsing Hua University, Taiwan, where I specialized in applying machine learning and deep learning techniques to solve complex molecular modeling challenges. My doctoral research focused on developing neural network potentials to accelerate structural sampling of carbohydrate molecules, providing valuable insights for experimental observations in IR spectroscopy, ion mobility spectrometry, and CID mass spectrometry.

My experience spans multiple neural network architectures and machine learning frameworks, including:

  • Neural Network Potentials: Designed and implemented custom models that significantly accelerated molecular structure prediction
  • Deep Learning: Practical experience with convolutional neural networks and graph-based neural network architectures
  • Generative Models: Worked with auto-regressive generative models for molecular structure prediction
  • Traditional ML: Applied algorithms such as Random Forest and XGBoost for classification and regression tasks
  • Computer Vision: Applied deep learning techniques to analyze complex structural data, with skills transferable to industrial fault detection

As a recipient of the Molecular Science and Technology Taiwan International Graduate Program Scholarship, I developed innovative computational methods that seamlessly integrated neural network potentials with structure sampling algorithms. This work demonstrated how AI can dramatically improve efficiency in scientific discovery processes.

I am now seeking opportunities to apply my expertise in AI and deep learning to computer vision applications in materials science, particularly for fault detection and quality control systems where my background in molecular structure analysis would bring unique perspective to industrial challenges.
